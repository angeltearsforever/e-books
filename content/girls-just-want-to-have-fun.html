for my friends

in cherry

blossom season in

my handheld

game i spent

a lot of

time catching

petals in a

net and tilting

my little head because

this is a game

where you get points for

wishing on a star they

gamified hope

and i bought it

and real space is

full of junk metal and dead dogs

imagine

rejecting something

then letting it circle you forever

couldn’t be me

it’s funny how the earth can’t

let anything go

i think that’s supposed

to be comforting

i can change

and if i want

what i had whatever

i used to be is always

there the text

the bad habit

hit save

every day i wake up and

think i’ll be better

and then i’m not it’s

fire season and

i want to tell you

i love you by talking

shit on someone else

my beautiful friends

every day

i wake up ruining

i didn’t know that i could

watch the end

of the world

from my house

through a digital

cloudless night

sometimes i ride

my bike at sunset

because a sunset

always looks like

an apocalypse

and because

presence is

its own rapture

when every moment

beats down the one

before it right

in front of you

i guess the joke is that we all

go out breathless

but i want

to be breathless

up the mountain when

we smoke our

separate joints and

i start to think we

might not be

so different from museums

that idea of eternity

defined by stasis like

those portraits of dogs

that went to space

on the mountain

it’s hard not to

look at the

dark and think

someone put us here to die

but if one

of us is sad we

drive to the valley or

we give each other tattoos or

talk about the moon or

drink in the kiddie pool

and all the museums are closed

but i can look at high-res

images online

of bernini’s ecstasy

of saint teresa
